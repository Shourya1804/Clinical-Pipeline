DISCHARGE SUMMARY

CHIEF COMPLAINT: SOB and chest pain.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
The patient is a 64-year-old male with a history of hypertension and type 2
diabetes mellitus who presented to the ED with acute shortness of breath. He
denies fever. There was no evidence of pneumonia on chest x-ray. We were asked
to rule out pulmonary embolism; a CT angiogram was negative for PE.

ASSESSMENT:
Possible congestive heart failure exacerbation. Acute kidney injury is unlikely.
The troponin was elevated, consistent with NSTEMI. No signs of stroke.

MEDICATIONS ON DISCHARGE:
1. Hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily
2. Metformin 1000 mg twice daily
3. Atorvastatin 40 mg nightly
4. Aspirin 81 mg daily

The patient was counseled and discharged in stable condition.
